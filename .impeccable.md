## Design Context

### Users

The app is for Sedona Method beginners who need a gentle, structured way to record topics, feelings, wants, actions, releases, and gains. It should support both quick mobile capture and longer focused practice sessions, with mobile as the primary context.

### Brand Personality

Quiet, focused, ritual-like, minimal. The interface should feel closer to a meditation app and a calm practice notebook than a commercial product, dashboard, or decorative wellness site.

### Aesthetic Direction

Use a restrained, breathable mobile-first interface with soft surfaces, clear hierarchy, and deliberate spacing. The current paper-and-ink direction can be refined or changed when it improves clarity. Avoid visual noise, sales-like polish, overly playful decoration, and dense desktop table patterns on phones.

### Design Principles

- Prioritize smooth, spacious input flows over showing everything at once.
- Keep record summaries compact and scannable; reveal detail only after selection.
- Use progressive disclosure for complex topic structures so nested forms do not crowd the screen.
- Preserve a quiet ritual feeling through restrained color, typography, and motion.
- Make touch targets generous and labels plain so beginners can act without interpreting the UI.
